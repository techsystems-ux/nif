Interior Design Courses Offered

BDes in Interior Design

Duration: 4 Years
The BDes program is designed for students who want deep understanding of interior design. Learning focuses on space planning, design thinking, and system based approaches. Students work on detailed projects and gradually build strong design foundations. The course also supports portfolio development over time.

BVoc in Interior Design

Duration: 3 Years
The BVoc program combines design basics with hands on studio work. Students learn practical interior design skills along with essential software training. Industry exposure is introduced to help students understand how interior projects are handled in real work settings. This program suits students who want applied learning.

MVoc in Interior Design

Duration: 2 Years
The MVoc program is meant for advanced learning and professional growth. Students focus on specialised interior design skills and practical application. The course supports deeper understanding of execution, coordination, and leadership within design projects.

MDes in Interior Design

Duration: 4 Years
The MDes program is designed for professionals aiming to refine their design thinking. Learning focuses on advanced concepts, innovation, and detailed project work. Students develop a stronger design perspective and prepare for leadership roles in interior design.

Career

Career Opportunities After Interior Design

Interior Designer

Graduates can work as interior designers on residential and commercial projects. The role involves space planning, material selection, and design execution. Designers work closely with clients and project teams.

Interior Decorator

Students interested in styling and dcor can work as interior decorators. This role focuses on finishes, furnishings, and visual appeal. Decorators help enhance spaces through colour, textures, and accessories.

Interior Stylist

Interior stylists work on curated spaces for shoots, displays, and presentations. Styling requires a strong visual sense and attention to detail. This path suits those interested in creative presentation work.

Design Consultant

Some graduates work as design consultants. They assist clients with layout planning, material choices, and design direction. This role requires clear communication and practical design understanding.

Freelancing and Independent Practice

Many interior designers choose to work independently. Freelancing allows designers to take on custom projects and collaborations. This path offers flexibility and creative control.
